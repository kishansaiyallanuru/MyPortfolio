MTD-HealthNet: Moving Target Defense for Hospital LANs

Designed and implemented a Moving Target Defense (MTD) framework to protect hospital local area networks from reconnaissance, lateral movement, and persistent cyberattacks. The system dynamically changes network parameters such as IP addresses and ports to reduce the attacker’s window of opportunity while ensuring uninterrupted communication for critical medical devices and compliance with healthcare regulations.

My Role & Contributions

  • Assisted in designing the secure hospital network architecture, focusing on segmentation and resilience against network-based attacks.
  • Implemented core components of the MTD Engine, including dynamic IP randomization mechanism.
  • Contributed to attack simulation and validation, assisting in performing reconnaissance and scanning attacks to evaluate MTD effectiveness.
  • Supported analysis of attack surface reduction and helped document security improvements achieved through MTD deployment.
  • Collaborated with the team to ensure operational continuity and regulatory compliance while deploying defense mechanisms.
